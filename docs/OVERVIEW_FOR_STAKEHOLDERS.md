# GASQ — Overview for stakeholders

*Non-technical guide: what this product is, who it serves, and how people use it.*

---

## What GASQ is

GASQ is an online platfoxrm for the **security services market**. It helps **organizations that need security** (buyers) connect with **security providers** (vendors), and gives both sides **tools to understand and compare costs**—hourly rates, staffing, patrol models, contract economics, and related calculators—so decisions can be grounded in numbers, not guesswork.

The platform also supports **posting jobs** and **responding with bids**, so procurement and vendor selection can happen in one place.

---

## Who it is for

| Audience | What they get |
|----------|----------------|
| **Buyers** | Post security needs, review vendor activity, run calculators to benchmark costs and contract scenarios, and purchase **credits** to use advanced tools. |
| **Vendors** | See opportunities, submit bids, and use the same calculators to prepare competitive, transparent proposals. |
| **Administrators** | Manage site settings, support users, and oversee **credits** and accounts where applicable. |

---

## What users can do (in everyday terms)

- **Create an account** and complete **phone verification** (a code sent by text message) so the account is trusted for sensitive features.
- **Explore marketing and informational pages** (pricing, FAQs, how the model works) without needing to be an expert.
- **Post a job** describing security work so vendors can respond.
- **Buy credits** when the buyer is ready to unlock full calculator access (payment is handled through standard online checkout).
- **Run calculators** that turn inputs (hours, posts, rates, patrol patterns, and similar) into **totals, comparisons, and breakdowns** useful for budgeting and negotiations.
- **Track account balance and history** of credit purchases and usage.

Some pages are visible to anyone; **full calculator access** follows the business rules below so the platform can stay sustainable and aligned with real job activity.

---

## How full calculator access works (buyers)

For **buyers**, using the full set of calculators is intentionally **step-by-step**:

1. **Verify the phone number** on the account.  
2. **Purchase credits**—these act like prepaid access for running the calculation tools.  
3. **Post at least one job** so the platform reflects real engagement before deep tooling is used.

**Vendors** are not required to post a job to use the calculators; they still need a verified account and sufficient credits where pricing applies.

The **cost per calculator run** (how many credits each run uses) is set by the organization operating the site and can be adjusted in configuration—not something end users need to configure.

---

## Why credits exist

**Credits** keep advanced analytics and calculators sustainable: they align heavy usage with fair support and infrastructure, and give buyers a clear path from **intent** (posting a job) to **analysis** (running tools). Users can **top up** credits when they need more runs.

---

## Trust, privacy, and support

- Phone verification improves accountability and reduces abuse of paid features.  
- Payment handling follows industry-standard practices for online checkout.  
- Organizations deploying this platform should publish their own **privacy policy**, **terms of use**, and **support contacts** to match their jurisdiction and business.

---

## Summary

**GASQ** is a **marketplace plus decision-support** product: jobs and bids bring buyers and vendors together, while **calculators and reports** help both sides speak the same language on **cost and scope**. Credits and verification ensure those tools are used seriously and fairly.
